# **XBD Collective — Brand Diagnostic Report**

**Audit Sources:** xbdesign.com \+ Instagram @xbd.collective\[[xbdesign](https://www.xbdesign.com/)\]

---

## **1\. THE PERCEPTION GAP (Evidence-Based)**

**What XBD claims vs. what the digital footprint delivers.**

XBD Collective positions itself as an award-winning, international, "boutique ethos, global perspective" firm operating across UAE, UK, Europe, Africa, and Asia, with landmark commissions — a 76-storey Rixos tower, St. Regis residences, Bvlgari Villa, W Residences on The Palm. By scope, complexity, and client caliber, this is a Tier 1 luxury architecture and interiors practice.\[[xbdesign](https://www.xbdesign.com/about/)\]

**The gap:** The website reads like a competent regional firm, not a global design authority. Evidence:

* The homepage hero is an auto-playing slideshow — the default presentation format of portfolio sites created in 2014\. Competing firms at this caliber (David Chipperfield, Ilse Crawford, Studio Zhu-Pei) use full-screen, cinematic, scroll-controlled immersive entry points with no navigation chrome visible in the first viewport.  
* The logo sits top-left in a fixed header at all times — small, static, and navigationally functional, but not spatially commanding. The infinity-loop mark has potential but is deployed at postage-stamp scale.\[[xbdesign](https://www.xbdesign.com/)\]  
* The About page is dominated by centered white-background copy and a single founder portrait. There is no visual narrative of process, materiality, or spatial philosophy — the things that separate a design mind from a contractor.\[[xbdesign](https://www.xbdesign.com/about/)\]  
* Awards are listed on a dedicated page with what appear to be blank image placeholders (award logos failed to load), suggesting poor asset management and low quality control on a page that should be a credibility anchor.\[[xbdesign](https://www.xbdesign.com/awards/)\]  
* Contact page is essentially blank — "OUR LOCATIONS" heading on an empty page — suggesting either a broken embed or unfinished implementation.\[[xbdesign](https://www.xbdesign.com/contact-us/)\]

**The gap in one sentence:** XBD is building 800,000 sq ft towers and dressing the portfolio in a Squarespace template.

## **2\. THEIR TASTE WORLD (Concrete Spatial Language)**

**The aesthetic universe XBD actually inhabits — read from their visual output.**

Looking across the project imagery on the site and Instagram feed:\[[instagram](https://www.instagram.com/xbd.collective/)\]

* **Material register:** Honed travertine, fluted limestone columns, book-matched marble with visible veining, brushed brass detailing, smoked oak millwork. Consistently warm-toned neutrals — cream, sand, warm grey — interrupted by jewel-tone accents (deep teal, burgundy, midnight blue) in hospitality contexts.  
* **Spatial typology:** Double-volume entrance halls with floating staircases. Floor-to-ceiling glazing framing waterfront or cityscape. Cluster-pendant lighting installations as focal centerpieces (visible across Rixos and the private cinema project). Pool-and-terrace interfaces that blur interior/exterior threshold.\[[instagram](https://www.instagram.com/xbd.collective/)\]  
* **Composition language:** Symmetry with deliberate off-axis breaks. Arched openings used as a formal device (KATA project). Organic curved furniture against rectilinear architecture — the tension between the structural grid and the lived curve.  
* **Lighting philosophy:** Layered — ambient cove \+ pendant drama \+ task concealment. They design with light as a spatial medium, not a utility (see Rixos chandelier installation, Solar House night shot).\[[xbdesign](https://www.xbdesign.com/)\]  
* **Scale register:** Monumental but not cold. The spaces feel commissioned for people who accumulate — collectors, hosts, connoisseurs. This is corroborated by their own Instagram taxonomy: "The Collector. The Host. The Entertainer. The Curator."\[[instagram](https://www.instagram.com/xbd.collective/)\]

**Taste world summary:** Late-modernist maximalism with editorial restraint. The reference coordinates are somewhere between a Amanresorts lobby, a Rem Koolhaas private residence, and a Parisian art dealer's apartment. This is important — it is not "modern luxury Dubai glitz." It is quieter, more referential, more layered.

## **3\. THE ENEMY (The Generic Standard They Are Trapped In)**

**The default visual category XBD currently lives inside.**

The enemy is the **Dubai Architecture Portfolio Site** — a recognizable genre characterized by:

1. **The hero slider.** Every slide reveals another render of a gleaming tower or marble lobby. No hierarchy. No story. No tension. You become immunized to the work within 8 seconds because every image is equally "impressive" and therefore equally forgettable.  
2. **Grid project thumbnails with project-name-on-hover.** The architecture equivalent of a product catalog. Althuraya Island, Solar House, Riviera — reduced to a 2x2 grid of images with floating serif text. The work deserves sequencing, not cataloging.\[[xbdesign](https://www.xbdesign.com/residential-type/architecture/)\]  
3. **The "Award-Winning" badge in paragraph one.** The About page opens: "XBD Collective is an award-winning international Architecture & Interior Design Consultancy Group…". This is so common in this category it has become noise. It signals insecurity, not authority.\[[xbdesign](https://www.xbdesign.com/about/)\]  
4. **Social media that explains the work instead of making you feel it.** Instagram captions like "AI can create a beautiful interior in seconds… but great design is about more than generating images" — this is a defensive stance, not a confident one. The brand is reacting to industry trends rather than setting them.\[[instagram](https://www.instagram.com/xbd.collective/)\]  
5. **The "London \- Dubai" location mark.** Displayed in spaced-out serif caps beneath the logo — a calling card of regional prestige signaling. Firms of actual global stature (Foster \+ Partners, Heatherwick) don't announce their offices in their logo lockup. The location becomes the brand rather than the work.\[[xbdesign](https://www.xbdesign.com/)\]

**The enemy in one sentence:** XBD is trapped inside the same visual grammar as 300 other Gulf-region design firms — the category camouflage is so complete that a client comparing studios cannot distinguish XBD from the next firm in the tab.

## **4\. PREMIUM LEAKS (Where the Digital Footprint Feels Cheap)**

**Specific moments where the experience undercuts the caliber of the work.**

1. **Awards page with broken images.** Three award logo boxes rendered as empty white rectangles. On a page whose entire purpose is to establish credibility with "90+ awards," broken image assets are devastating. This is a trust leak at the highest-stakes touchpoint.\[[xbdesign](https://www.xbdesign.com/awards/)\]  
2. **Contact page with no visible content.** The contact page loaded to show a header, "OUR LOCATIONS" in light gray text, and nothing below. Whether this is a lazy-load failure or an unfinished page, the result is the same: a potential client who clicks "Contact" meets a blank wall.\[[xbdesign](https://www.xbdesign.com/contact-us/)\]  
3. **Footer credit: "Designed by E8."** Every page of the site closes with a visible "Designed by E8" attribution link. This is a standard web agency watermark that communicates: someone else built this for us. A brand charging premium project fees for design authorship cannot afford to announce their website was handled externally via a visible footer credit. The cognitive dissonance — *we design exceptional spaces / here is our vendor's name at the bottom* — is real.\[[xbdesign](https://www.xbdesign.com/about/)\]  
4. **Instagram follower-to-post ratio.** 750 posts, 24.1K followers. For a studio with this project caliber (Bvlgari, W Hotel, St. Regis, Rixos), this is low authority signal. It suggests the social strategy is volume-posting renders rather than building a distinctive editorial voice that attracts organic audience growth.\[[instagram](https://www.instagram.com/xbd.collective/)\]  
5. **Carousel-inside-hero on mobile.** The homepage hero is a multi-image slideshow with nine pagination dots visible at the bottom. On mobile, this collapses into an unfocused stack of renders with no clear entry hierarchy. Premium architecture sites use a single, held, full-bleed image or a controlled cinematic scroll — not a dot-navigated gallery.\[[xbdesign](https://www.xbdesign.com/)\]  
6. **Typography system: inconsistency under scrutiny.** On-site body copy is set in mixed weight serif with widely-tracked caps for headings ("OUR STORY," "PRESTIGIOUS AWARDS," "FOUNDER"). The tracking creates visual air but also visual blankness — no typographic texture, no system, no scale hierarchy beyond H1 vs. body.

## **5\. CORE VISUAL DIRECTION (What the New Spatial Website Should Look Like)**

**The spatial brief for the next iteration.**

The new XBD website should feel like entering the antechamber of a great building — before you understand it fully, you already feel it is serious.

**Structural principles:**

**A. One project at a time, held.** The entry experience should be a single, full-screen, high-resolution photograph of a completed space — no text, no navigation chrome, no slides. The image holds for 3 seconds then dissolves to the next. The user enters on XBD's terms, not their own browsing habit.

**B. Scroll as spatial reveal.** Moving through the site should feel like moving through a sequence of rooms. Each section transition uses a controlled vertical reveal — not a slide, not a fade, but a spatial unmasking. The viewport becomes the aperture.

**C. Typography with weight and restraint.** A single display typeface — ideally a contemporary geometric serif (something in the lineage of Canela or Freight Display) — used at large scale with breathing room. No tracked caps for everything. No mixed weights without intentional reason. The type should feel selected by the same intelligence that selects materials.

**D. Navigation as object, not utility.** The hamburger or minimal icon menu is half right — but it should be even more reduced. A single icon, top-right, that expands into a full-screen navigation with featured project imagery as the background. Visiting the menu should feel like making a choice, not opening a drawer.\[[xbdesign](https://www.xbdesign.com/)\]

**E. Project pages as spatial essays.** Each project page should open with a brief, precisely-written spatial statement — one paragraph, not three — followed by a curated sequence of photography (not a gallery grid). The photography should move from establishing shot → material detail → human scale → atmosphere. No renders mixed with photography without clear designation.

**F. Color system: material-honest neutrals \+ one chromatic anchor.** The site palette should mirror the material register of the work: warm limestone white (\#F2EDE6), dark charcoal (\#1C1C1C), with one selective accent — perhaps the dark teal present across their interiors — used only for interactive states and focal moments.

**G. About as spatial biography.** The founder section should not be a headshot and title. It should be a short, first-person spatial manifesto — where Ellen Søhoel's design philosophy is articulated in the language of space, not corporate biography — accompanied by one portrait and one process image (a working model, a material sample board, a pencil sketch over a plan).

**H. No footer credit. No auto-play video. No star ratings.** These are the three immediate removals that signal seriousness.

**The new site in one sentence:** A studio that designs spaces worthy of silence should have a website that knows when to stop speaking.
