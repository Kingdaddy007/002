# Visual Motion Analysis: Haven
**Status:** Reference Material
**Purpose:** Extracting cinematic motion mechanics for the XBD Collective Pitch Concept.

---

## 1. The Hero Reveal (The Typography Takeover)
**What Haven Did:** The hero is massive typography and white space. As the user scrolls, the text moves up slightly, but a full-screen video slides up from *behind* the text and expands to 100vw/100vh, completely swallowing the typography.
**How we steal it for XBD:** 
- Instead of starting with an image (like Zorgen), we could start with massive text: **"THE REALITY MAKER"** or just **"XBD"**. As Ellen scrolls, the breathtaking night shot of Solar House slides up from *behind* the text and expands to fill her entire screen. This creates a massive "curtain reveal" moment.

## 2. Pinned Scrub Sequences (The Material Focus)
**What Haven Did:** Pinned a central 16:9 container on the screen. As the user scrolls, the content inside the container scrubs rapidly through detailed images (inspecting wood, light fixtures). 
**How we steal it for XBD:**
- We need to prove intimacy for the UHNWI client. Between Solar House (Residential) and Rixos (Hospitality), we can pin a container and scrub through 3-4 macro shots of their materials (marble cutting, brass, blueprints). It proves they are builders, not just 3D renderers.

## 3. The Asymmetrical Parallax Grid (Solving the "Range" Problem)
**What Haven Did:** Used extreme whitespace and an asymmetrical masonry grid where every image moves at a different vertical speed based on scroll.
**How we steal it for XBD:**
- The other AI suggested a "filmstrip" to show their other projects. A filmstrip shrinks the work. Instead, we use this Haven-style Parallax Grid. We drop 4 or 5 massive photos (Opus Tower, Kata Restaurant) into a sea of white space. They move at different speeds as you scroll. It proves range without feeling like a catalog.

## 4. The Transition Architecture (Physical Layering)
**What Haven Did:** Very few hard cuts. Instead, the next section physically slides *over* the top of the previous section, creating a z-index layered feel, like pulling physical cards over each other.
**How we steal it for XBD:**
- When transitioning from Solar House to Rixos, Rixos doesn't just fade in. It slides up and *covers* Solar House, acting as a physical floor. It makes the website feel like a tactile object.
