# Visual Motion Analysis: AIR Business Center
**Status:** Reference Material
**Purpose:** Extracting cinematic motion mechanics for the XBD Collective Pitch Concept.

---

## 1. The Typographic Split (Hero Parallax)
**What They Did:** Massive letters "A", "I", "R" sit on the screen. As the user scrolls down, the letters split and move horizontally to the edges of the screen, making way for the building render to slide up from the bottom.
**How we steal it for XBD:** 
- Instead of "A I R", imagine the letters "X B D" occupying the center of the screen. As Ellen scrolls, the "X" moves far left, the "D" moves far right, and the "B" fades out. In the massive gap they leave behind, the night shot of Solar House pushes up from the bottom via a `clip-path` mask. This is a highly physical, structural opening move.

## 2. Horizontal Typographic Impact
**What They Did:** Massive text ("THE MOMENTUM" and "TO RISE HIGHER") slides in horizontally from the left and right edges, clipping into view exactly when they meet in the center.
**How we steal it for XBD:**
- If we want to use a strong piece of copy (like the "Reality Maker" positioning), we don't just type it on the screen. We have the words slide in from opposite horizontal edges on scroll, colliding in the center to form the sentence. It adds kinetic energy.

## 3. The Pinned "Detail" Slider
**What They Did:** A two-column setup for the lobby. The image wipes horizontally while a number ticks up (1 / 2 -> 2 / 2), driven by scroll.
**How we steal it for XBD:**
- If we want to show multiple angles of the Rixos lobby, we don't use a carousel arrow. We pin the section. As she scrolls, the first image smoothly wipes horizontally to reveal the second angle, proving depth without requiring her to click anything.

## 4. Bridging Hard Cuts
**What They Did:** They cut aggressively between pure white and pure black backgrounds. To prevent it from looking like a mistake, they always have an image container overlap the threshold between the two colors.
**How we steal it for XBD:**
- We noticed this in Zorgen and Normal Is Boring as well. Hard cuts are the standard for luxury architecture sites. When we transition from Solar House to Rixos, we will overlap the imagery across the color change boundary to pull the user's eye down smoothly.
