# Visual Motion Analysis: Normal Is Boring
**Status:** Reference Material
**Purpose:** Extracting cinematic motion mechanics for the XBD Collective Pitch Concept.

---

## 1. The Typographic Preloader
**What They Did:** The site opens with pure typography. The text "scrambles" and decodes before resolving into the headline.
**How we steal it for XBD:** 
- The decoding text feels slightly too "tech/agency" for a luxury architecture firm. However, the idea of a purely typographic opening (no photos immediately) aligns perfectly with the "Curtain Reveal" we discussed in the Haven transcript.

## 2. The Full-Screen Horizontal Scroll (The Filmstrip Solution)
**What They Did:** To show multiple projects, they pinned the screen and forced vertical scrolling to move horizontally across massive, full-height project cards. Crucially, as the cards move left, the images inside pan right (inner parallax) to create a window-pane effect.
**How we steal it for XBD:**
- We have a massive debate right now about how to show XBD's "Range" without shrinking their work into a cheap carousel. 
- *Option A (From Haven):* A masonry grid with lots of white space.
- *Option B (From Normal Is Boring):* This massive Horizontal Scroll. This is incredibly elegant. It allows us to show Opus Tower, Kata Restaurant, and other masterplans at 100vh scale, packing a ton of massive imagery into one fluid scroll motion without making the page vertically endless.

## 3. Typographic Parallax
**What They Did:** Treating text as physical objects. Words in the hero section move independently on the Y-axis as the user scrolls.
**How we steal it for XBD:**
- When we display XBD's core values or an architectural statement, we don't just fade the text in. We have the words slide up at slightly different speeds, giving the typography physical weight.

## 4. Hard Wipes & Color Flow
**What They Did:** Exclusively scroll-triggered hard wipes (e.g., a pure black div sliding entirely over a pure white div).
**How we steal it for XBD:**
- This confirms the transition logic we saw in Haven. To maintain a high-end, editorial feel, we avoid soft, cheap gradients. When Solar House (night/dark) transitions to Rixos (day/bright), it should be a hard, physical wipe over the screen.
