## THE CORE ANGLE: THE DREAM CAR STRATEGY

Do not show them something different.
Show them the best version of what they were already trying to do.

The same car they chose. The same projects they picked. The same hero, the same entry.
But the version they could never have imagined when they built what they built.

When someone sees the best version of something they already wanted — they do not feel criticized.
They feel understood. And then they feel the gap.

That gap, felt in the gut, is what makes them respond.

---

## THE PSYCHOLOGICAL MECHANISM

**Step 1 — Recognition.**
They land on something that looks like their world. Their projects. Their work. Not our portfolio. Not our ideas. Theirs.

**Step 2 — Elevation.**
But it looks the way it always should have looked. Solar House is not in a carousel. It is not competing with an office business center. It is given the full weight it deserves.

**Step 3 — The Gap Lands.**
They do not need us to explain the problem. They feel the distance between what they have been showing the world and what their work actually is. That feeling is not comfortable. It creates urgency.

**Step 4 — Curiosity Opens the Door.**
They want to know: who built this? How did they know? Can this be the actual website?

That question is the deal starting.

---

## WHAT WE ARE NOT DOING

- We are not telling them their website is broken.
- We are not listing their problems.
- We are not sending a diagnosis report as an outreach message.
- We are not showing off animation or technical capability for its own sake.

All of those close doors. The Dream Car strategy opens one.

---

## THE ANCHOR PROJECT: SOLAR HOUSE

Solar House is the right choice for the pitch entry because:

- It is completed and built — not a render. Proof, not promise.
- The photography is strong — dramatic night shot, glass, light, pool. Cinematic material already exists.
- It is a private luxury home — the UHNWI individual register, the most valuable ICP to speak to.
- It is slide 2 of 9 in their current hero. It is being wasted. Showing it correctly is the most direct way to make the gap visible without saying a word.

---

## WHAT THE PITCH SHOULD FEEL LIKE TO ELLEN

She opens it.
She sees Solar House.
Not rotating. Not numbered. Not competing.
Just — held. The way a great photograph deserves to be held.

She scrolls.
The space moves with her. Slow. Controlled. Like walking into the house.

She feels something she has not felt looking at her own website.

She thinks: *"This is what we are."*

And then she wants to know who did this.
