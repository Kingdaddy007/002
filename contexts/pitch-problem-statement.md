# PITCH PROBLEM STATEMENT
**Type:** Anchor file — private strategic reference
**Status:** Locked
**Rule:** Every decision in this pitch must serve this problem. If it doesn't, it's out.

---

## THE ONE PROBLEM

**XBD's digital presence fails to reflect the reality of their work.**

They have built Solar House in Emirates Hills. They have Rixos, Bvlgari-level commissions.
Real work. Completed. Photographed. Proven.

It lives in a 9-slide auto-playing carousel where it gets 3 seconds of screen time before being replaced by an office business center in the Opus Tower.

The work is Tier 1. The presentation is not.
That gap is the problem we are attacking.

---

## WHY WE CHOSE THIS PROBLEM (AND NOT THE OTHERS)

They have many problems — broken awards page, blank contact, generic copy, E8 footer credit, off-brand Instagram. We are ignoring all of those for now.

We chose this one because:

1. **It is solved by showing, not arguing.** We do not write a report. We build something and they see it. The pitch IS the proof.
2. **It uses their own work.** We are not inventing their brand or telling them they are wrong. We are taking Solar House and treating it the way it deserves to be treated. That is not a critique. That is a gift.
3. **It creates instant recognition.** When they see their own project presented correctly, their reaction will not be "interesting idea." It will be "that is our work — how did they do that?"
4. **It is the most honest entry point.** Every other problem (copy, contact form, Instagram) is downstream of this one. Fix the presentation first and everything else becomes a conversation they want to have.
5. **It is achievable in a pitch.** We cannot fix their broken links in a concept. But we can show one project — one space — in a way that proves the gap is real and that we can close it.

---

## WHO AT XBD WE ARE TRYING TO TRIGGER

**Ellen Søhoel. The founder.**

Not a marketing manager. Not an account director. The creative authority.

The person who chose those projects for the homepage. The person who saw Solar House completed and approved its photography. The person who knows — instinctively — what the work deserves to look like.

When Ellen sees Solar House shown correctly, she does not think about pricing or timelines. She thinks: *"This is what we should have been."*

That thought is the door opening.

---

## WHAT THIS PITCH IS NOT

- It is not a full redesign proposal.
- It is not a critique of what is broken.
- It is not a portfolio of our own work.
- It is not a price list or a service menu.

It is a demonstration of understanding. One problem. One project. Shown once, correctly.
The rest of the conversation follows from there.
