## 1. The Generic Layout to Destroy
If a generic WordPress template or a weak AI built this site, it would look like this:
1.  **Hero:** A full-width slider cycling through 5-6 random renders of living rooms, with a generic sans-serif overlay saying *"XBD Collective — Luxury Interior Design & Architecture in Dubai."*
2.  **About Section:** A block of text containing generic corporate jargon (e.g., *"We deliver bespoke luxury solutions, striving for excellence and innovation..."*) next to a picture of the founder, Ellen Søhoel.
3.  **Services:** A 3-column icon grid showing: Architecture, Interior Design, Project Management.
4.  **Portfolio Grid:** A standard 3x3 photo gallery that opens images in a lightbox, showing Althuraya, Kata, and other projects with no narrative or text.
5.  **Awards:** A wall of logo badges.
6.  **Contact:** A standard contact form with generic fields (Name, Email, Subject, Message) and a large, bright teal "Send Message" button.

---

## 2. Banned Patterns & Design Gaps
We ban the following elements to prevent this site from feeling like a generic real estate or corporate page:
*   **Banned Colors:** No primary neon teals, generic blues, or flat corporate greys.
*   **Banned Typography:** No standard system serifs (like Times New Roman) or system sans-serifs (like Arial) used without weight/tracking discipline. No mid-sized headers that clutter the hierarchy.
*   **Banned Navigation:** No thick, sticky white header bar that eats up screen space and breaks the full-bleed visual immersion.
*   **Banned Animation Presets:** No universal bounce animations, simple opacity fades, or basic page-slide-ups that look like template bootstrap sites.
*   **Banned Buttons:** No loud, rounded SaaS-style CTA buttons (e.g., "Book a Consultation"). 

---

## 3. The "Industry Swap" Test
*Rule:* If the layout, colors, and styling can be swapped to represent a luxury hotel, a high-end furniture brand, or a developer company without breaking the design, then the site has failed. 

*XBD Specificity:* The site must belong *exclusively* to an elite, architectural design atelier. It must show the tension between raw material (concrete, steel frames, limestone blocks) and the finished, tailored luxury objects (custom joinery, fine marble, soft lighting).
