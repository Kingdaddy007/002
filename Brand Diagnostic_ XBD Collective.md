# **Brand Diagnostic: XBD Collective**

## **1\. Perception Gap**

The brand wants to be perceived as an ultra-luxury, internationally authoritative architecture and interior design collective (London/Dubai), but the current footprint feels like a dated corporate real estate template because it relies on hijacked non-native scrolling, mixes uncontextualized 3D renderings with real photography, dilutes founder authority with an endless grid of staff, and suffers from broken links ("Whoops, Our Bad") on core trust pages like Awards.

## **2\. Taste World**

**Current Signal:** Corporate developer showroom meets 2010s Flash-era portfolio.

**Desired World:** Hospitality-grade private home and monumental modern craft.

* **What Belongs:** Immersive, high-resolution photography of *completed* spaces; deep, editorial case studies; quiet, sophisticated typography; intuitive and native vertical scrolling; stark visual separation between built environments and masterplan concepts.  
* **What Never Belongs:** Hijacked scroll bars, horizontal carousels without context, generic 404 error pages, overwhelming grids of 50+ team members that scream "agency overhead" rather than "curated studio," and unlabelled 3D renderings masquerading as finished projects.

## **3\. Audience & Desired Identity**

* **Audience:** UHNWI (Ultra-High-Net-Worth Individuals), luxury real estate developers, and hospitality groups operating in high-leverage markets like Dubai and London.  
* **Desired Identity:** The sophisticated, scale-capable global design partner that bridges European refinement with Middle Eastern monumentalism—handling everything from initial architecture to the final curated object.

## **4\. Founder / Creative Authority**

Currently, the creative authority is buried. The "Our Story" page relies on generic corporate copy ("award-winning international... bespoke... cutting-edge") and a single isolated founder photo, which is immediately overshadowed by a jarring, massive wall of black-and-white employee headshots.

* **The Fix:** The site must lead with the *philosophy* of the collective. Founder lore and partner authority must replace the "HR directory" feel. Show the leaders in the space, interacting with materials, or sketching out masterplans.

## **5\. Enemy and Category Rejection**

* **The Enemy:** Overdesigned Spectacle & Contractor Utility.  
* **The Counter-Standard:** The old world says luxury is proven by showing an army of staff, 100 award badges, and forcing users into a slick, non-scrolling carousel to view projects. This studio believes that true luxury is quiet, frictionless, and proven through the built work itself. That means the site must show spatial mastery through deep, contextualized case studies and effortless usability—removing all UX gimmicks so the architecture speaks for itself.

## **6\. Signature Method / Language**

The current site simply lists "Architecture, Interior Design, Landscape, Masterplan" as disconnected silos. There is no narrative tissue connecting them. The brand needs a language that articulates how they transition seamlessly from monumental exterior scale to intimate interior touch.

## **7\. Proof Architecture**

* **Current State:** Spray-and-pray. The portfolio is a confusing horizontal slider. The narrator rightly points out, "I don't even understand if this is architecture or interior."  
* **The Fix:** Implement fewer, stronger projects. Present them as editorial case studies. Radically separate "Concepts/Unbuilt" from "Completed Works." High-net-worth clients want proof of execution, not just proof of 3D rendering skills. Show project constraints, material decisions, and transformations.

## **8\. Premium Signals & Leaks**

* **Premium Leaks (Urgent):** \* Hijacked scrolling (frustrating the user immediately).  
  * The "Whoops, Our Bad..." 404 error page when clicking on major awards (destroys trust).  
  * "Loading..." text stuttering on the team grid.  
  * Generic data points without narrative ("Status: Concept Design").  
* **Premium Signals (To Implement):**  
  * Restraint in UI chrome. Let the imagery bleed to the edges.  
  * Quiet, confident inquiry flow (a curated contact experience, not a generic form).  
  * Press and client proof presented cleanly without chaotic logo walls.

## **9\. Spatial Visual Direction**

* **Entry Atmosphere:** A full-bleed, beautifully lit, slow-moving video or high-res image of a *built* space. No interface text covering the focal point.  
* **Room Sequence:** Standard, buttery-smooth vertical scrolling. Allow the user to "walk" down the page naturally.  
* **Typography Posture:** Confident, highly legible serif for editorial storytelling, paired with a stark, quiet sans-serif for technical details (square footage, location, status).  
* **Proof Format:** Asymmetric grids that pair wide architectural shots with macro details of interior materials (marble, brass, textiles).

## **10\. Motion Posture**

The current motion is jerky, forced, and disorienting (horizontal slides masking as page loads). The new motion posture must be **Calm**. Fade-ins on scroll, gentle parallax on large images, and absolutely no hijacking of the user's native trackpad or mouse wheel. The UI should step back, letting the architecture hold the weight.
